Hoverboard Motor Teardown

I bought a replacement motor for a hoverboard on ebay for about $35 shipped. I did a quick analysis of some of its specs to determine its potential for higher power output. I also needed a really cheap and fairly tame sensored motor as a test mule for the brushless motor controller I want to build.

Specs:

  • Mass: 3185.6g
  • 27N30P configuration
  • Phase resistance: 0.3 Ω
  • Kv: 18 rpm/V
  • Stator dimensions: 105mm OD, 29mm length
  • No load loss: 31.5V*0.68A = 21.4W at 559rpm

I only got one no load point because the Castle Creations HV80 controller I was using didn't record any current for the 22.2V test. Thus, I have to model the parasitic losses linearly i.e. I can't account for eddy current losses rising with the square of rpm.

Based on these findings, assuming that the motor can dissipate 200W of heating which I think is a pretty conservative estimate, this motor should be able to make about 2700W continuously at 147V, 18.3A. For a 7lb motor, that isn't great, especially considering the REALLY (for an ebike) high voltages necessary to balance the copper and parasitic losses. However, 2700W is great for a $35 motor. With a rewind for higher Kv, this could be a great motor, but the necessity of a rewind makes the price a little less attractive.
